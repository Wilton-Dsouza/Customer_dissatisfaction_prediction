Outline-

When a consumer chooses not to use a company's services anymore, that is called customer turnover. 
Businesses regularly analyze their customer turnover since knowing which customers are on the verge of leaving is incredibly beneficial to them. 
The goal of this project is to leverage the data already available to train a machine learning model 
that will accurately anticipate which customers are going to leave a firm, enabling the business owner to make more informed marketing decisions

Reasons why Customer churn happen:

1. Poor Customer Experience: According to a study by PwC, 32% of customers would stop doing business with a company they loved after one bad experience.
2. Lack of Personalization: A survey by Epsilon found that 80% of customers are more likely to do business with a company that offers personalized experiences.
3. High Prices: A study by McKinsey found that price sensitivity is increasing across all industries, with 49% of customers switching brands due to price.
4. Changes in Customer Needs: A survey by Salesforce found that 64% of customers expect companies to adapt to their changing needs and preferences.
5. Competitors' Offers: A study by Accenture found that 82% of customers would switch brands due to better service or prices.
6. Negative Publicity: According to a survey by Edelman, 55% of customers would stop doing business with a company they perceived as unethical.
7. Lack of Innovation: A survey by Forrester found that 47% of customers would switch brands if a company fails to provide innovative products or services.
